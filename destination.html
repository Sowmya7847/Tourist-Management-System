Explore Indian Destinations

Taj Mahal, Agra

An Immortal Symbol of Love. The Taj Mahal, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, is renowned for its stunning white marble architecture.

Red Fort, Delhi

Symbol of Mughal Dynasty. The Red Fort in Delhi is a historic fortification and a significant landmark of Mughal architecture, known for its massive red sandstone walls.

Golden Temple, Amritsar

Holiest Sikh Shrine. The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is the most sacred site in Sikhism, famous for its stunning golden architecture and serene ambiance.

Amer Fort, Jaipur

A Majestic Hilltop Fort. The Amer Fort, located on a hill in Jaipur, is a beautiful example of Rajput architecture, known for its stunning views and elaborate design.

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

A Five-Storeyed Historic Landmark. The Hawa Mahal, or Palace of Winds, is a unique structure with intricate lattice work, designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals discreetly.

Humayun's Tomb, Delhi

The Tomb of Mughal Emperor Humayun. Humayun's Tomb is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and the first garden-tomb on the Indian subcontinent, influencing the design of the Taj Mahal.

Qutub Minar, Delhi

An Ancient Victory Tower. The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the tallest brick minarets in the world, built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193.

Mysore Palace, Mysore

The Opulent Residence of the Wadiyars. Mysore Palace is a grandiose structure known for its exquisite architecture and rich history, serving as the residence of the Wadiyar dynasty.
